Our Story

A Royal Home by the Ganga

Ganga Heritage stands on Bhimgoda Road, a short walk of a few hundred metres from the ghats of Har Ki Pauri — close enough that the evening Ganga Aarti bells reach the balconies. What began as a modest riverside guesthouse has been reimagined as a palace-inspired residence, its corridors and rooms detailed in the vocabulary of Rajasthani royalty: turned jharokha windows, carved jaali screens, and gleaming brass fittings in place of anything cold or modern.

It is a house built for pilgrims and travellers alike — for the family arriving at dawn for a holy dip, and for the traveller who wants a regal balcony, a good thali, and a view of the river at rest.

The Satvik Kitchen

Food Cooked in Devotion, Not Just in a Kitchen

In keeping with the sanctity of Har Ki Pauri, our kitchen follows the satvik tradition — purely vegetarian, free of onion and garlic on request, and prepared fresh each service rather than held or reheated. It is food meant to keep the body light and the mind clear, the way pilgrims have eaten by this river for generations.

  • Pure vegetarian thalis, with a no onion-garlic option for those observing vrat or satvik diet
  • Seasonal, local produce sourced fresh each morning
  • Milk-based sweets and prasad-style offerings, made in-house
  • Meals cooked to order — nothing pre-made or held in advance
Location

A Few Steps from Har Ki Pauri

The hotel sits on Bhimgoda Road, within easy walking distance of the ghats, the evening Aarti, and the old bazaars of Haridwar.

Har Ki Pauri Ghat
